São Paulo City Guide

Everything you need for a great day off in SP.

Market
Mercado Municipal

A lively market filled with fresh fruits and endless snacks to try. Let out your inner foodie and spend a morning here.

Coffee
Coffee Lab

São Paulo is home to some of the best coffee in Brazil, and Coffee Lab leads the charge. A must-visit for any caffeine lover.

Park
Parque Ibirapuera

São Paulo's version of Central Park. Stroll through gardens and visit the contemporary art museums for a more relaxing day off.

Neighborhood
Vila Madalena

Colorful streets packed with street art, quirky shops, and the famous Beco do Batman. Stop at any corner bar for a caipirinha or ice cold Antartica Cerveja.

Neighborhood
Liberdade

São Paulo's Little Tokyo — lantern-lined streets leading to hole-in-the-wall noodle shops, dessert stalls, and some of the best Japanese food outside Japan.

Los Angeles City Guide

Coffee, drinks, and restaurants worth going out of your way for.

☕ Coffee
The Boy and The Bear

Multiple locations across the city. Colombian-sourced coffee with a wide range of brewing options.

Dayglow

Takes coffee enthusiasts on a global journey, featuring beans from renowned roasters worldwide.

Madlab

Science-forward coffee bar for those who want to geek out on extraction and brewing methods.

Dinosaur Coffee

A neighbourhood staple in Silver Lake. Low-key vibe, great espresso, and always a good crowd.

Stereoscope

Korean-influenced specialty coffee with beautiful interiors. Multiple locations — the Newport Beach original is worth the trip.

🍷 Drinks
Natural Wine
Good Clean Fun

A low-key natural wine bar in DTLA with an impressive selection and a genuinely welcoming vibe. One of the best in the city.

Natural Wine
Tabula Rasa

A Silver Lake staple with an ever-changing list of natural and minimal-intervention wines. Small plates, great atmosphere.

Bar
El Chucho

A natural wine dive bar in Mar Vista with a pool table, a great back patio, and a vibe that's hard to find anywhere else in the city.

Brewery
Highland Park Brewery

One of LA's most respected craft breweries. The taproom in Chinatown is a great spot to spend an afternoon.

Brewery
El Segundo Brewing

A South Bay institution known for their hop-forward IPAs. The taproom has a laid-back beach town energy that's hard not to love.

Brewery
Firestone Walker Venice

The Venice outpost of the legendary Central Coast brewery. Great beers, great food, and a massive space right in the heart of the action.

Cocktails
Tiki Ti

A legendary LA dive bar that's been slinging tiki cocktails since 1961. Tiny, cash only, and completely irreplaceable.

Cocktails
The Normandie Club

One of the best cocktail bars in the country, tucked into a Koreatown hotel. The drinks are technical, the vibe is effortlessly cool.

Bar
Gold Line

A no-frills neighbourhood bar in Highland Park with strong pours, good vinyl, and a music-friendly crowd. The kind of place you end up staying until last call.

🍽️ Food
Breakfast
Bluey's

An Australian-influenced breakfast spot with locations in Santa Monica and Mar Vista. Excellent avocado toast, great coffee, and a relaxed neighborhood energy.

Breakfast
République

A stunning all-day bakery and restaurant in a historic Mid-City building. Weekend breakfast here is one of the great LA experiences — go early.

All Day
Great White

Australian-style all-day café with multiple locations across the city. Reliable, well-executed, and always a good crowd.

Dinner
Dunsmoor

Wood-fired American cooking in a warm, rustic space in Glassell Park. One of the most exciting restaurants to open in LA in recent years.

Dinner
Marvin

A French-inspired wine bar and restaurant on Beverly Boulevard. Effortlessly cool, great natural wine list, and food that punches well above its weight.

Dinner
Budonoki

Japanese comfort food in Virgil Village. The katsu and ramen are exceptional — a neighbourhood gem that flies slightly under the radar.

Dinner
RVR

A lively Japanese-inspired izakaya in Venice with an excellent drinks program and small plates built for sharing. Low-key atmosphere, high-quality food.

Steak
Matu

A focused steak restaurant built around New Zealand wagyu. If you're going to splurge on dinner in LA, this is the move.

Tacos
Sonoratown

Sonoran-style flour tortilla tacos made fresh to order. Simple, perfect, and one of the most beloved taco spots in the city.

Tacos
Holbox

Seafood-focused tacos in Mercado La Paloma. The aguachile and tostadas are exceptional — a must for anyone who loves Mexican coastal cooking.

Tacos
Mariscos Jalisco

A legendary East LA food truck famous for their crispy shrimp tacos dorados. An LA institution — find the truck, get in line.

Copenhagen City Guide

Coffee, drinks, food, and things to do on a day off in CPH.

🎡 Things To Do
Amusement
Tivoli Gardens

The third-oldest operating amusement park in the world, right in the middle of the city. Let out your inner child.

Spa
Aire Ancient Baths

Thermal baths in a beautifully designed space. The perfect way to decompress after a run of shows.

Culture
Freetown Christiania

A self-governing neighbourhood unlike anywhere else. Make sure to find your way over there — the subway and ferry system make it easy to navigate.

☕ Coffee
La Cabra

One of the world's most respected roasters. Minimalist, precise, and exceptional. Multiple locations in the city.

Coffee Collective

A Copenhagen institution with a deep commitment to ethical sourcing. Some of the best filter coffee in Europe.

April Coffee

Beautifully light, fruit-forward roasts from one of the newer names making waves in the Copenhagen coffee scene.

🍷 Drinks
Natural Wine
Rosforth & Rosforth

A glass of wine under a bridge in Copenhagen. Sounds odd, looks incredible, tastes great.

Beer
Mikkeller Baghaven

A world-famous craft brewery right on the harbour. Excellent beer in a stunning waterside setting.

Top Pick
La Banchina

Sauna, swimming, and drinking combined into one harbour spot. The ultimate Copenhagen day off experience when the weather cooperates.

🍽️ Food
Food Hall
Torvehallerne

Wander the halls of this covered food market — you're bound to find anything you're looking for. A great first stop.

Dinner
Baka D'Busk

For the veggie lovers — a dinner here is a must. Creative, seasonal, and genuinely one of the best plant-based spots in the city.

Street Food
Reffen

An outdoor street food market on the harbour. On a nice day this should absolutely be in the cards — great vibes, great variety.

Breakfast
Apotek 57

Don't sleep in too late — home to some of the best pastries and breakfast dishes you'll ever find in Copenhagen.

Breakfast
Atelier September

A bright, airy café beloved by locals. The avocado toast is iconic but everything on the menu is worth ordering. Don't miss the pastries.

Sydney City Guide

Coffee, drinks, food, and things to do on a day off in Sydney.

🏊 Things To Do
Pool
Bondi Icebergs

Swim in the iconic ocean pool, sweat it out in the sauna, hit the gym, or relax at the onsite café — all for under $10. The best way to brush off jet lag.

A 6km clifftop walk past secluded beaches and ocean pools. Stretch your legs and get some sun — one of the best urban walks in the world.

Multiple bars right on the water. A reliable spot for a drink outside when the Sydney weather cooperates — which it usually does.

☕ Coffee
Single O

A Sydney institution now with locations in Japan too. Known for their Hazel automatic milk dispenser and consistently excellent filter coffee.

Skittle Lane

Multiple locations across the city. A tucked-away gem in the CBD. Great espresso and a vibe that somehow makes you feel like a local immediately.

Will & Co

Multiple locations across Sydney. Friendly, consistent, and one of the most reliable specialty coffee options in the city.

🍷 Drinks
Natural Wine
Love Tilly Devine

The best natural wine bar in Sydney. A tiny, legendary spot in Darlinghurst that helped put Australian natural wine on the map.

Beer
Bitter Phew

An excellent craft beer bar with a wide range of taps. Knowledgeable staff and a relaxed atmosphere — the kind of place you stay longer than planned.

🍽️ Food
Breakfast
Social Brew Cafe

Consistently great breakfast in a lively setting. A Surry Hills staple with quality ingredients and excellent coffee.

Lunch
Mr. Wong

A grand Cantonese restaurant in the CBD. Dim sum at lunch is one of the great Sydney eating experiences.

Lunch
Fishmongers

Fresh fish and chips steps from Bondi Beach. No-frills, perfectly executed, and exactly what you want after the coastal walk.

Lunch
Lox Stock & Barrel

A beloved deli and café in Bondi. The smoked salmon bagel is iconic — expect a queue but it moves fast.

Dinner
Nomad

A wood-fired restaurant in Surry Hills that showcases exceptional Australian produce. Treat yourself to a dinner experience to remember.

Best Parks On Tour

Places to get outside and reset between shows. Bring a book, get off your phone.

New York City
Governor's Island

Car-free island in New York Harbor with sweeping views of the Manhattan skyline. Perfect for a bike ride or a long walk on a day off in the city.

Los Angeles
Griffith Park

4,000+ acres of trails and green space above LA with iconic views of the Hollywood sign. Hike to the observatory and decompress.

Sydney
Bondi to Coogee Coastal Walk

A 6km clifftop walk along the coastline past secluded beaches and ocean pools. One of the best urban walks in the world.

Copenhagen
Kastrup Sea Bath

A stunning wooden pier and sea bath designed by architects. Swim in the Øresund strait and watch planes land at the airport nearby.

Amsterdam
Vondelpark

Amsterdam's most beloved park — grab a coffee, rent a bike, find a bench. Locals and tourists coexist here in perfect harmony.

Tokyo
Yoyogi Park

A massive green escape in the heart of Tokyo. Weekends bring street performers, picnickers, and the occasional cosplay crowd. Calm on weekdays.

Singapore
Gardens By The Bay

The supertree grove is stunning at night. Worth going twice — once during the day for the gardens, once at dusk for the light show.

Mexico City
Bosque de Chapultepec

One of the largest city parks in the Western Hemisphere. Home to the Anthropology Museum — easily a full day off well spent.

Travel

Hotel Hacks

Sleep better. Travel smarter. Feel more like yourself on the road.

Sleep
Thermostat

Your body sleeps best in a cool environment, between 65-68°F. Turn it down the moment you check in and give it time to drop before you get into bed. One of the highest ROI sleep habits with zero effort.

Sleep
Electrical Tape

Smoke detector LEDs, alarm clocks, TV standby indicators — hotel rooms are full of small lights that disrupt melatonin production and fragment sleep. A small roll of black electrical tape covers them all in under a minute. Pack it once, use it forever.

Sleep
Sleep Mask

Blackout curtains in hotels are never fully blackout. Light bleeds in from the sides, the hallway, the city outside. A well-fitted sleep mask eliminates the variable entirely. Non-negotiable for anyone after a late show night.

Sleep
Ear Plugs

Corridor noise, thin walls, street traffic, early housekeeping — hotels are loud. Foam ear plugs cost almost nothing and eliminate a significant portion of the noise that fragments sleep on the road. Keep a stash at all times.

Gear
Power Strip

Hotel rooms are designed for one or two guests with one device each. On tour you have a phone, laptop, watch, earbuds, and camera all needing charge simultaneously. A compact power strip solves this permanently. Get one with USB-A and USB-C ports built in.

Gear
Dedicated Toiletry Kit

Keep a toiletry bag permanently packed and never unpacked at home. Everything you need, in travel sizes, always ready to go. You save time packing and never forget anything. Set it up once and maintain it.

Laundry
Detergent Pods

A single pod in the sink with warm water and fifteen minutes handles a surprising amount of laundry. Socks, underwear, a base layer — hang dry overnight and ready by morning. Your own clothes, with detergent you like, without hotel laundry prices.

Comfort
Bluetooth Speaker

A hotel room that sounds like yours is a hotel room that feels like yours. Music while you shower, wind-down audio before bed, something playing while you unpack. A compact waterproof speaker is one of the simplest quality of life upgrades for life on the road.

Coffee
Aeropress

Hotel in-room coffee is universally bad. An Aeropress is compact and produces genuinely good coffee in under three minutes. Bring your own coffee from home or source local beans on a day off. Worth it every single morning.

Health & Wellness

Tips For Healthy Touring

Small habits that compound. Science-backed ways to feel better on the road.

Movement
Walk After Meals

A 10-minute walk after eating blunts the blood sugar spike that follows a meal, particularly important on tour when food timing is irregular. Taking stairs over elevators adds low-intensity movement that accumulates meaningfully across a run of shows. Both habits require zero equipment and zero scheduling.

Nutrition
Skip After Show Food

Eating late at night, particularly the low quality food that appears after shows, disrupts sleep architecture and delays recovery. Your body prioritises digestion over restoration when you sleep on a full stomach. Even a light snack is significantly better than a full meal. When possible, eat before the show.

Movement
Flying Safer

Cabin pressure and prolonged sitting reduce circulation significantly. Compression socks counteract this by maintaining venous return in the lower legs. Standing and walking every 60-90 minutes reduces DVT risk measurably. Cabin humidity sits around 10-20%, well below what your body needs. Drink consistently throughout the flight, not just when you feel thirsty.

Circadian
Jet Lag Protocol

Light is the most powerful signal your circadian clock responds to. Getting bright light exposure in the morning of your new timezone and avoiding bright light in the evening accelerates adaptation faster than any supplement or medication. Even 20 minutes outside makes a measurable difference. Eating on the local meal schedule from day one also helps anchor your circadian clock faster. Resist the urge to eat at your home timezone hours.

Mental
Mindfulness

Five minutes of intentional stillness, whether a formal meditation or a gratitude list, is clinically shown to reduce cortisol, improve emotional regulation, and build resilience against the chronic low-grade stress of touring life. The format matters less than the consistency. Apps like Waking Up or Headspace lower the barrier to entry significantly.

Mental
Digital Boundaries

Constant connectivity is one of the defining stressors of modern touring. Designating phone-free windows, during meals, the hour before bed, or the first 30 minutes of your morning, measurably reduces baseline anxiety and improves presence both on and off the road. The notifications will wait.

Mental
Staying Connected

Maintaining relationships outside the tour bubble is one of the strongest predictors of long-term mental health on the road. The insularity of touring life is real. A deliberate weekly call with someone who knows you off the road provides perspective and grounding that the bubble can't. Don't let the time zone difference become an excuse.

Hearing
Protect Your Ears

Noise-induced hearing loss is the most common occupational injury in live music, and it's cumulative, irreversible, and entirely preventable. Even brief exposure at high SPL compounds over a career. Custom molded earplugs attenuate evenly across frequencies so you still hear clearly, just safely. If you haven't had a hearing test recently, get one. Your ears are your livelihood.

Circadian
Get Morning Sunlight

Natural light exposure within 30 minutes of waking triggers a cortisol pulse that sets your circadian clock for the day, improving alertness, mood, energy, and sleep quality that night. Even overcast daylight is orders of magnitude brighter than indoor lighting. Five to ten minutes outside is enough. It's one of the highest ROI habits on this entire list.
